Differential Equations

A differential equation is a mathematical equation that relates a function with its derivatives. Differential equations are used to model various physical systems, such as motion, heat, electricity, and fluid dynamics. They can describe how a quantity changes over time or space.

Types of Differential Equations

Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)

Involves functions of a single variable and their derivatives. For example:

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = ky \)

Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)

Involves functions of multiple variables and their partial derivatives. For example:

\( \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = c^2 \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \)

Solving Differential Equations

Separation of Variables

This method involves rearranging the equation to separate the variables on either side of the equation. For example:

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = ky \)

Rearrange to:

\( \frac{1}{y} dy = k dx \)

Integrate both sides:

\( \ln|y| = kx + C \)

Solve for \( y \):

\( y = Ce^{kx} \)

Integrating Factor

Used for first-order linear ODEs of the form \( \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x) \). The integrating factor is \( e^{\int P(x) dx} \).

Characteristic Equation

For linear differential equations with constant coefficients. For example, for \( ay'' + by' + cy = 0 \), assume a solution \( y = e^{rt} \) and solve the characteristic equation \( ar^2 + br + c = 0 \).

Example: Solving an ODE

Solve the first-order ODE:

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = y \cos(x) \)

Step 1: Separate the variables

\( \frac{1}{y} dy = \cos(x) dx \)

Step 2: Integrate both sides

\( \int \frac{1}{y} dy = \int \cos(x) dx \)
\( \ln|y| = \sin(x) + C \)

Step 3: Solve for \( y \)

\( y = e^{\sin(x) + C} \)
\( y = Ce^{\sin(x)} \)
