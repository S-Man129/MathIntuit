Conic Sections

Conic sections are the curves obtained by intersecting a plane with a double-napped cone. The angle of the plane relative to the cone determines the shape of the intersection. The primary types of conic sections are circles, ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas.

Types of Conic Sections

Circle

A circle is formed when the plane is perpendicular to the axis of the cone.

\( (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \)

Here, \((h, k)\) is the center, and \(r\) is the radius.

Ellipse

An ellipse is formed when the plane is angled such that it intersects both nappes of the cone but does not pass through the apex.

\( \frac{(x - h)^2}{a^2} + \frac{(y - k)^2}{b^2} = 1 \)

Here, \((h, k)\) is the center, \(a\) is the semi-major axis, and \(b\) is the semi-minor axis.

Parabola

A parabola is formed when the plane is parallel to a generator of the cone.

\( y - k = a(x - h)^2 \)

Here, \((h, k)\) is the vertex, and \(a\) is a constant that determines the width of the parabola.

Hyperbola

A hyperbola is formed when the plane intersects both nappes of the cone in such a way that the angle is steeper than the angle of the cone.

\( \frac{(x - h)^2}{a^2} - \frac{(y - k)^2}{b^2} = 1 \)

Here, \((h, k)\) is the center, \(a\) is the distance between the vertices and the center along the transverse axis, and \(b\) is the distance between the vertices and the center along the conjugate axis.

Applications of Conic Sections

  • Orbits of Planets: Ellipses describe the orbits of planets and satellites.
  • Reflective Properties: Parabolas are used in the design of telescopes and satellite dishes due to their reflective properties.
  • Architecture and Engineering: Hyperbolas are used in structures like cooling towers and suspension bridges for their structural properties.

Example

Find the equation of a parabola with vertex at \((1, 2)\) and focus at \((1, 5)\).

The distance from the vertex to the focus is \( |k - k'| = 3 \). The parabola opens upwards because the focus is above the vertex. The standard form of the equation is:

\( (x - h)^2 = 4p(y - k) \)

Here, \( (h, k) = (1, 2) \) and \( 4p = 12 \) (since \( p = 3 \)).

So, the equation is:

\( (x - 1)^2 = 12(y - 2) \)
