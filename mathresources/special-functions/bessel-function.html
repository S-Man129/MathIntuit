Bessel Function

Bessel functions are canonical solutions to Bessel's differential equation:

\( x^2 \frac{d^2 y}{dx^2} + x \frac{dy}{dx} + (x^2 - n^2)y = 0 \)

where \( n \) is a real number, often an integer. These functions are named after Friedrich Bessel and are essential in many fields such as wave propagation, static potentials, and quantum mechanics.

Types of Bessel Functions

Bessel Functions of the First Kind \( J_n(x) \)

These are solutions to Bessel's equation that are finite at \( x = 0 \) for non-negative integers \( n \).

\( J_n(x) = \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^m}{m! \, \Gamma(m+n+1)} \left( \frac{x}{2} \right)^{2m+n} \)

For example:

\( J_0(x) = \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^m}{(m!)^2} \left( \frac{x}{2} \right)^{2m} \)

Bessel Functions of the Second Kind \( Y_n(x) \)

These are solutions to Bessel's equation that have a singularity at \( x = 0 \). They are also known as Neumann functions or Weber functions.

For non-negative integers \( n \):

\( Y_n(x) = \frac{J_n(x) \cos(n\pi) - J_{-n}(x)}{\sin(n\pi)} \)

Applications of Bessel Functions

  • Electromagnetic Waves: Bessel functions appear in the solutions of the wave equation in cylindrical coordinates.
  • Heat Conduction: They describe the radial part of the temperature distribution in a cylindrical object.
  • Vibrations: They are used to model vibrations of circular membranes.

Example

Consider solving the differential equation:

\( x^2 \frac{d^2 y}{dx^2} + x \frac{dy}{dx} + (x^2 - 4)y = 0 \)

This corresponds to \( n = 2 \). The general solution is:

\( y(x) = A J_2(x) + B Y_2(x) \)

where \( J_2(x) \) and \( Y_2(x) \) are Bessel functions of the first and second kind, respectively, and \( A \) and \( B \) are constants determined by boundary conditions.
